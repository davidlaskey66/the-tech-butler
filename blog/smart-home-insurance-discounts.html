Can Smart Home Technology Lower Your Home Insurance?

Published March 28, 2026 • 5 min read

Smart home devices are typically discussed in terms of convenience, security, and peace of mind. There is another benefit that gets less attention: potential home insurance discounts.

Canadian insurers are increasingly recognizing that certain smart home technologies reduce the risk of claims — particularly water damage, break-ins, and fire. Some offer explicit discounts for verified smart home devices. Others use the presence of these systems as a factor in underwriting without advertising a specific rate reduction.

This is not a guarantee of savings, and the specifics vary significantly by insurer and province. But it is worth understanding which devices are most likely to be recognized and how to approach the conversation with your insurance provider.

Water Leak Detection

Water damage is one of the most common and costly home insurance claims in Canada. A slow leak under a sink, a failed water heater, or a burst pipe during a cold snap can cause tens of thousands of dollars in damage — much of which could be prevented with early detection.

Smart water leak sensors placed under sinks, near water heaters, and beside appliances send an immediate alert to your phone when moisture is detected. Some systems connect to an automatic water shutoff valve that stops the flow of water before significant damage occurs.

Several Canadian insurers — including Intact, Aviva, and others — offer discounts specifically for verified water leak detection systems. The discount range varies, but 5 to 15 percent on the water damage portion of your premium is common where programs exist. Some insurers require professional installation for the discount to apply.

Smart Smoke and CO Detectors

Standard smoke detectors are required by building code. Smart detectors go further — they alert your phone immediately when triggered, regardless of whether you are home, and some connect directly to monitoring services that can contact emergency services on your behalf.

Insurers generally view interconnected, monitored smoke detection systems more favourably than standard detectors. If your smart detectors are connected to a professional monitoring service, this is worth disclosing to your insurer as it may qualify for a discount similar to a traditional alarm system.

Security Systems and Cameras

Professionally monitored security systems have qualified for home insurance discounts in Canada for decades. Smart home security systems — cameras, door and window sensors, motion detectors connected to a monitoring service — fall into the same category for most insurers.

The key distinction for insurers is typically whether the system is professionally monitored. A Ring or Nest camera system that sends alerts to your phone is valued differently than the same system connected to a 24/7 monitoring service that can dispatch police if needed. If maximizing insurance savings is a goal, a monitored system is worth the additional monthly cost.

Smart Locks

Smart locks are not consistently recognized as an insurance discount factor on their own, but they contribute to an overall security posture that some insurers evaluate holistically. A home with a smart lock, security cameras, and a monitored alarm system may qualify for a better rate than a home with only one of these elements.

Vacation Properties

For vacation properties in the Collingwood and Blue Mountains area, smart home technology can have a more significant impact on insurability. Properties that sit vacant for extended periods are considered higher risk by most insurers — they are more likely to experience undetected water damage, break-ins, or maintenance issues.

Smart thermostats with freeze alerts, water leak detection, cameras, and regular property check-ins all reduce the risk profile of a vacant property. Some insurers require a minimum check-in frequency for vacant property coverage — a smart monitoring system that provides documented property status can supplement or reduce the required check-in frequency.

How to Approach Your Insurer

Contact your insurance broker or provider directly and ask specifically whether they offer discounts for smart home devices, monitored alarm systems, or water leak detection. Be specific about what you have installed — the category of device matters more than the brand in most cases.

Keep documentation of installed devices — purchase receipts, installation records, and model numbers. Some insurers require this to verify a discount. If your devices were professionally installed, this documentation is more straightforward to provide.

Shopping your insurance at renewal with your smart home installations documented is worth doing even if your current provider offers no discount — other insurers may price the risk differently.
